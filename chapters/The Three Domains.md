# The Three Domains: Empirical, Actual, Real

Critical realism holds that reality is *stratified* into three domains that are ontologically continuous (part of one reality) but analytically distinct:

- **The Empirical** — everything we can observe, experience, or measure: the realm of direct perception and documented evidence.
- **The Actual** — everything that happens, whether or not anyone observes it: all events and processes, including those that leave no empirical trace.
- **The Real** — the underlying structures, mechanisms, and causal powers that generate actual events, including powers that exist even when they are not currently being exercised.

Causal power resides in the Real: it generates events in the Actual and makes observations in the Empirical possible. The causal arrow therefore runs Real → Actual → Empirical, which is why adequate explanation must move in the opposite direction — from observed patterns back through actual events to the mechanisms that produced them.
